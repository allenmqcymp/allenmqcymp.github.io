About Me

left: My surname “Ma” (literally means “horse”) in Chinese.
center: Muriwai, New Zealand - source: my iPhone.
right: Miller Library, Colby College - credit: Huân Bùi

I am Chinese, although I’ve lived my whole life in Auckland, New Zealand. Currently, I’m a Junior at Colby College, a small college in New England, USA. I’m studying Computer Science and Math, expecting to graduate with my Bachelors in 2021.

An avid learner, I enjoy programming, DIY, solving puzzles, and learning new languages.

I am a native speaker in both Mandarin Chinese and English, and can speak some broken Japanese. There are so many languages and cultures that interest me, and one of my long-term goals in life is to live and work for a while in a non-English speaking country. If I had a superpower, I would definitely want to clone myself so that I could live simultaneously in multiple countries and learn multiple languages at the same time.

I also love to stay active, and do so by trail running (I dislike road-running because it hurts my joints), hiking, lifting weights, and playing ultimate frisbee.

I am deeply interested in how we can use technology to benefit our lives. With the rise of automation, I often find myself thinking about how humans, regardless of profession, can leverage technology in useful and harmonious ways, rather than viewing it as a threat. My future direction, although vague, is to explore how the human experience and emergent technologies can mesh together productively.
